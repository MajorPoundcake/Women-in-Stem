Organizations that Support Women in Stem

American Association of University Women (AAUW)

Originally the "Association of Collegiate Alumnae", AAUW was founded in 1882 to help women access a college education, improve educational standards, and increase opportunities for women both their education and careers. The former association supported Marie Curie in her radium experiments as well as others seeking refuge during World War II.

Association for Women in Science (AWIS)

Founded in 1971 to advocate for equal pay, nondiscrimnation in STEM envirnoments, recognition of the achievements of female scientists, and empowering women to obtain leadership roles and achieve a better work-life balance.

Million Women Mentors

Is a STEMconnector initiative focused on encouraging girls and women alike to remain in STEM jobs, providing educational and professional development to more than 40 states, while working with the U.S. State Department, international non-profit organizations, and postsecondary schools to close the gender gap in stem (international resources available).
