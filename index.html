Welcome to Women in Stem!

When you think of innovators within the technological or scientific fields, who or what comes to mind? Maybe you're picturing a cartoon character, like Dexter from Dexter's Laboratory or maybe Professor Utonium from the Power Puff Girls? Then again, maybe you're picturing a real person, like Albert Einstein or Nikola Tesla? Regardless of what or who you visualized, did your visualization happen to be of a man?

Don't worry, it's normal when people are asked to do this exercise that they're only able to think of men as examples, and that isn't a reflection of education or them as an individual, but it does highlight how underrepresented women's contributions to society are historically, given some of the biggest historical accomplishments in both medicine and technology can be credited to a woman.

My aim for this website is to introduce you to some of the accomplishments made by Women in Stem (WISTEM), to highlight some of the challenges women face within the these fields and the importance of representation, the importance of addressing the gender gap and its benefits, as well as provide some additional resources for women continuing their education with an interest in STEM.
