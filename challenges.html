Challenges

Many experts in the field attribute the lack of women in science-related fields to a lack of representation. Some of the challenges presented are as follows:

Stereotypes
We know from the practice on the homepage that many associate the field of science with men.
Lack of role models and mentors
Despite mention of some great female figures on the innovators page, there's an underwelming number of women in leadership positions advocating for these fields.
Unconscious bias
Unconscious bias is defined as "the deep-seated prejudices we all absorb due to living in deeply unequal societies".
Work-life balance imbalance
Often women have to choose between the wellbeing of their families and their careers, especially if companies do not provide accommodations for mothers and have demanding hours.

Global Gender Gap (2023)

Despite efforts to attract more women to the sciences (not including medical) in recent years, the amount of women in STEM has decreased with time.

How can we help?

Encouragement
Encouraging young girls and women to pursue an education in STEM, as well as being a part of the supportive environment encouraging them to stay despite their own unique set of obstacles.
Inclusive Workplaces
Creating environments where we hold one another accountable by identifying unconscious biases within our respective institutions and promoting inclusivity.
Role Models
Highlighting women within companies and prioritizing closing the gender gap by hiring and promoting women.
Opportunities
Hosting networking events and offering paid trainings and mentorships can help women develop experience and confidence needed to advance in STEM fields.
